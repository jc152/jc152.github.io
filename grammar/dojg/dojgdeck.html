DoJG anki deck

  • set up
  • styles

The DoJG deck allows you to review the contents of the Dictionary of Japanese Grammar through Anki. Download it here.

How to use

By default, the deck includes two types of card for each grammar point:

  • Cloze deletion: The front of the card shows a sentence with a grammatical term blanked out, alongside a description of that term's meaning. You have to fill in the blank.
  • Recognition: One of the Japanese example sentences is on the front of the card in full. Depending on the style you choose, the grammatical explanation may be on either the front (default) or the back (sentence/random styles below).

You may want to suspend one of these two types depending on your preference (either will be effective so long as you read the example sentences):

  • On the Anki start screen, click Browse.
  • Select the DoJG deck from the left list.
  • Click Cards...
  • There are "recognition" and "cloze" tabs at the top; click X on the type you want to delete.

Styles

To change styles:

  • On the Anki start screen, click Browse.
  • Select the DoJG deck from the left list.
  • Click Cards...
  • Replace the template/styling for each card type (tabs at top) with the appropriate field below.

The styles available are:

  • Core Exposure [Default]: Cloze and exposure (answer on front) cards.
  • Core Sentence: Cloze and sentence (answer on back) cards.
  • Core Random: Cloze and sentence cards. Instead of testing on the first example sentence every time, a random sentence is picked. This encourages you to memorize the grammar point rather than the sentence.
  • Classic: Cloze and exposure cards. The original style.
  • Anon Random: For sentence cards only. Between 1 and 6 sentence are randomly selected for the front of the card. Unlike the Core Random style above, the sentences will remain consistent across devices per day. Source.
